  • Things I loved to do in Rome

    As the weather gets colder, and we approach finals my memory drifts back to one year ago. One year ago I remember being so anxious about leaving for Rome. I had to get my Visa, copy all my credit cards and passport, and worry about to pack. Now a year later I long to have those worries because I miss Italy so much. I decided to make a list of what I loved doing most while I was there, maybe it will come in handy for someone else visiting for the first time, or for me when I become old and my memories fade. So here’s the list, in no particular order:

    1. Get to know the city- take a tour of the historical district, get off at every metro stop and take a look around, take a path you’ve never traveled on a free day.
    2. In the Spring buy a euro bottle of wine go to Piazza Navona and enjoy the jazz and people watching
    3. Order Suppli at every restaurant you go to, it’s heaven in a bite
    4. Explore the Ghetto then eat at Da Ghetto: a little pricey but the best Jewish Italian food around, tell the waiter you’re price point and let him pick the bottle (he won’t cheat you, it’s his job to know good food and wine).
    5. Grab an English newspaper from campus and pretend to be super scholarly at Caffe Greco (Via Candoffi)
    6. Spend a Sunday at Azzurro Scipioni cienima (Via degli Scipione, 82) and watch La Dolce Vita- don’t let Sylvia inspire you too much or else the policia at the Trevi fountain will only be too happy to serve you with a 70 euro fine
    7. Enjoy the art of the city, there is no where else like it- go inside churches, look at the frescos on buildings, check out a modern art exhibit
    8.  Take the hike up the stairs by Gianicolo and enjoy the Vista at Villa Pamphilli. If you go at sunset there is a bar with wine that you can sip and talk with your date as you look over the eternal city
    9. Check out Via di San Giovanni in Laterno during the day (one of the staple churches of Rome) and at night (hottest gay street in Rome) the difference is literally night and day.
    10. On a cold day try a bag of roasted chestnuts from a street vendor.
    11. Take a bus and visit the Mausoleum of Constantina I found it interesting to see such early Christian Iconography in Constantin’s daughter’s burial place- make it a game and see how much you can spot.
    12. On early morning dawn your big sunglasses and pretend to be Audrey Hepburn in Breakfast at Tiffanys as you window shop the extravagant on Via Condotti.
    13. As you’re waiting for a bus to termini or the metro to Trastevere say hi to the kittys at Argentina.
    14. Bring out your little kid and go inside Little Big Town (a toy shop near Victor Emmanuel Monument) there is always bubble and people dressed in costumes.
    15. If feeling home sick try going to Open Door Bookshop (Via della Lungaretta, 25)- they buy and sell second hand books in English.
    16. Pick up a copy of of the Magazine “Where in Rome” (found on campus) to learn about all the events happening in Rome that month<don’t want you to miss out on what you don’t know>
    17. Take one of the many many transportation strikes as a chance to wander aimlessly through the city, you never know what you might find.
    18. Eat at Alfredo e Ada (Via dei Banchi Nuovi 14) this place is literally a hole in the wall but some of the best food I had in all of Italy. There are only like 5-6 tables and yo only get two options but for less that 20 euro you get amazing food <includes wine, apetizer, meal, desert, water> but remember only open Mon-Fri
    19. On a chilly day try Rivendita di Ciocolato e Vino (Vicolo del Clinque) where you can drink hot coco in a chocolate cup!
    20. Get dressed up (super classy) and go to the ballet- We saw swan lake and it was amazing!
    21. Try to try every piazza flavor (it’s cheap and good—-best is in Naples).
    22. Pack a picnic, and rent a bike and explore Villa Borghese- the park is huge and even has a zoo so it may take a couple days (the park above Popollo)
    23. Get wrapped up in the magic of modern Italian at  Il Bucaro <Via delgli spagnoli 27> (enjoy a great meal that may hurt your wallet a bit).
    24. Try your luck at lying like Gregory Peck at La Bocca della Verita- don’t worry know the legend says it was broken in the middle ages when a cunny cheating woman outsmarted the mouth of stone. She had her lover pretend to be a mad men going around kissing innocent women, and when it was her turn at the judgment mouth she was spared because she said, “I’ve only ever kissed my husband and that mad man there.”
    25. Become comfortable with the often crazy homeless performers on Pointe Sisto on a weekend night.
    26. Get blessed by the Pope every Sunday at noon.
    27. Have the best amatricana at il Ragno d’oro (Villa Silla, 26 /near Vatican).
    28. Get a delicious dinner and THE BEST TIRAMISU in Roma at Cacio e Pepe (Vicolo de Clinque) hopefully you get my friend Sergio the best waiter ever!
    29. Indulge in the black truffle ravioli and the fiori friti at Il Duca (Vicolo de Clinque)
    30. Decide between 80 different gelato flavors at Gelateria della Palma (Via della Maddenla 20/ near Pantheon and Trevi fountain).
    31. Missing the parties back home, play a game of ruit at The Drunken Ship (Campo di Fiori).
    32. Relax with Hooka and tea at Shanti’s (Via Fabio Massimo, 68).
    33. Enjoy a good espresso or cappuccino at your local bar, while you’re there enjoy a croissant filled with creme or jelly.
    34. Try not to get to silly with the $16 mixed drink pitchers at Sloppy Sams and Drunkin Ship (Campo di Fiori).
    35. Get on the metro get off at the Lepanto stop walk down Viale Guilo Cesare towards the river at then about 3 blocks up you will see a pink lighted bakery get the BEST CANOLIS I’VE EVER EATEN! Also if you go on Sunday they have a sweet antique market on the traffic circle.
    36. Take a walk down Viale di Trastevere and take advantage of the street shopping.
    37. On a Sunday get off at the second tram stop before the Trastevere Train station walk down to Via Portuense and explore the huge flea market but keep a firm hold on your wallet.
    38. On a sunny warm day walk over to Trastevere Island and sunbathe on the concrete shore.
    39. Buy something from my favorite European chain Zara, my favorite of the stores is 2 stories and on Via del Corso
    40. Si hi to the music man and fire throwers at S. Maria in Trastevere at night, if you arrive during the day go inside the church there is a wonderful medieval fresco.
    41. Take the challenge of the stairs to reach the top of Capitoline Hill inside you’ll find the most beautiful chandeliers.
    42. Exit the church and take more stairs up until you reach the top of the Birthday Cake, a great view of the city and a yummy lunch (Victor Emanuel Monument).
    43. Race across Circo Massimo with a worthy opponent.
    44. Enjoy the Wonders of a pyramid amidst an Italian city.
    45. Take the metro next to the pyramid and spend a Saturday at the beach, 30 min from Rome.
    46. Visit the Colosseum and the Forums and spring for a guide otherwise honestly it’s just a bunch of old rocks.
    47. Get familiar with Termini so you can easily travel by train through Italy with ease (they have self service stations in English where you can easily buy your tickets).
    48. People watch in Popolo.
    49. Take multiple Italian cooking classes.
    50. Learn how to taste good wine at a wine tasting class.
    51. Get a freshly roasted cup of coffee at San. Estacio ( near Pantheon) some of the best chocolate covered espresso beans.
    52. If in Florence try the salted caramel gelato at Grom (Via delle Oche 24a)
    53. Also in Florence have dinner at Trattoria Marione (Via della Spada, 27) I wish I could steal their recipe for Ribollita.
    54. Tour a vineyard or multiple vineyards in Tuscany.
    55. Go to  a Tuscany spa where you can swim in natural hot springs.
    56. If in Perugia find Pizzeria Etrusca  (via Ulisse Rocchi 29/31) and try their pizza with egg…sounds weird but so delicious.
    57. If in Parma savor the flavor of prosciutto in hot bread pockets.
    58. In Sciliy swim in the ocean in March
    59. Take advantage of every API trip they offer, and do all they suggest.
    60. When the flowers begin to bloom go to Tivoli and walk among the fountains.
    61. Practice your Italian you can get more out of your trip.
    62. I know its cheesy but go to Montepulciano and have a bottle of local wine then reenact the scene from twilight when Edward tries to expose himself to the public.
    63. Buy your groceries fresh from Piazza San Cosimato know that your ingredients are always fresh and in season.
    64. Visit Venice during Carnival.
    65. Bus2alps can be a cheap way to travel, nut remeber its a bus so no long trips.
    66. Ryan Aire is cheap so you can fly anywhere between them and Easy Jet (beware the hidden fees).
    67. Buy a couple pieces of staple clothing in Italy, so when you get compliments when you receive the inevitable compliments and you can say ” oh this I picked it up while living in Rome.)
    68. Try carcoffi (articokes) fresh and fried
    69. Go to the opera, but read the libretto in English first online.
    70. Don’t leave Rome without throwing a coin into the Trevi fountain to make sure you get to come back.

  • Chef Andrea Consoli’s recipe for: Tiramisù

    4. Dessert: Tiramisù
     
    Ingredients:
    - 4 eggs
    - 4 tablespoon of confectioner sugar
    - 250gr mascarpone cheese
    - ladyfingers or savoiardi biscuits
    - 200ml /2 cups espresso coffee
     
    Instructions:
    Make some coffee to dip in your lady fingers and set aside. To make the cream mixture you have to separate 4 eggs yolk from the whites, and use an electric whisk to whip 4 eggs whites with 2 spoons of confectioner sugar and a few grains of salt until stiff but not dry. Then into another bowl you have to beat the 4 yolks and 2 spoons of confectioner sugar until very thick and light in color. With a wooden spoon, stir in 250gr of mascarpone cheese (it’s an imported product in the States, so I’m using the Italian measurement so you will know how much to get!) until smooth. As soon as both of the creams are ready, you have to mix them from the bottom to the top with a spatula……in this way your egg whites will keep their consistency. To assemble, dip half of the ladyfingers, one at a time, in the coffee mixture and line a long flat serving dish with them. Spoon a layer of the cream mixture over these. Add another layer of dipped ladyfingers and some chocolate chips, then spoon the remaining cream over the top. We prepared only one layer of ladyfinger, even if the quantity of cream was enough to make two layers. Cover the whole thing with a thick layer of grated unsweetened chocolate/cocoa powder and leave it chilling in the fridge for min. 2 hrs. If you want, this dish can be made up to one day in advance, and in this case sprinkle cocoa powder only before serving and not in advance. It will be delicious!!

  • Recipe for: Chicken scaloppini with prosciutto, cheese and sage

    3. Second course: Chicken scaloppini with prosciutto, cheese and sage (Scaloppine di pollo con prosciutto e salvia) ***This is Greg’s favorite!!
    Ingredients for 4 servings:
    - 4 boneless chicken breasts, thinly sliced
    - all purpose flour
    - 4 slices prosciutto
    - 4 slices soft, mild and easy to melt cheese (the one I bought for you is called Edamer)
    - 4 leaves fresh organic sage
    - 1 glass dry white wine
    - 2 tbsp unsalted butter
    - juice of 1 lemon
     
    Instructions:
    Place the flour on a plate and dip the chicken in the flour, ensuring that each piece is evenly coated. Shake gently to remove any excess flour. Melt the butter in a large frying pan over medium-high heat. Add the prosciutto and sautè until crisp, about 2 minutes. Add the chicken in a single layer, making sure the pieces don’t overlap. Sautè until brown on one side, about 3 minutes. Then switch side, add a leaf of sage, the the cheese and the crispy prosciutto on top. Squeeze the juice of a lemon, that will add the citrus taste to it and then add the wine and cook until evaporated for about 5 minutes. Add salt and simmer until the meat is cooked through, about 2 minutes. Transfer to a heated serving dish. Drizzle the sauce over the chicken and serve hot.
